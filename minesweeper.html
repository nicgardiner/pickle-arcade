// HOW TO PLAY

Objective

Reveal every safe cell on the grid without detonating a mine. Use numbered clues to deduce where mines are hidden, and flag them to keep track.

Reading the numbers

A revealed number tells you exactly how many of the up to 8 surrounding cells contain mines. A 1 next to a corner cell with only one hidden neighbour means that neighbour is definitely a mine. Cross-referencing several numbers is how you deduce safe cells.

Tips

• Your first click is always safe — mines are placed after you click.
• The mine counter (top-left) decreases as you place flags.
• Corners and edges are often good starting points — fewer neighbours means faster deduction.
• When stuck, use probability: cells adjacent to many high numbers are more likely mines.

Controls

Left Click
Reveal a cell. If it's a mine — game over. If it's empty, nearby safe cells open up automatically.
Right Click
Place or remove a 🚩 flag on a cell you believe contains a mine. Flagged cells cannot be accidentally revealed.
Double Click
Chord — if a revealed number already has the correct number of flags around it, double-clicking it clears all remaining neighbours instantly.
Long Press
On touch screens, hold a cell to flag it instead of revealing it.
R
Reset the current board instantly.
Esc
Close any open dialog.
